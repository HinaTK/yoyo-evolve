# Investment Rules

## Stable Rules
- When evidence is thin or conflicting, downgrade to `watch_only`.
- Every actionable recommendation must include a clear invalidation condition.
- Prefer theme confirmation through ETFs before upgrading a single-stock thesis.
- Treat headline-driven moves without follow-through as lower-confidence setups.
- Label short-term timing confidence separately when posterior evidence shows early-window misses.
- For `avoid` calls, state near-term bounce risk separately from medium-term downside thesis.
- Before clustered `avoid` calls on correlated names, require a broad/ETF rebound-risk check; if unavailable, use `watch_only`.
- If T+3/T+5 error repeats while T+10/T+20 works, downgrade timing confidence before changing the thesis.
- Do not treat posterior evaluation as current-session confirmation.
- After repeated bullish misreads, do not upgrade low-pass-rate symbols to `buy_candidate`, `hold`, or `accumulate` without broad-market and ETF confirmation.
- After repeated defensive misreads, do not issue `avoid` on rebound-prone or low-pass-rate symbols without live downside confirmation and broad/ETF rebound-risk evidence.
- On selective risk-on days, do not upgrade rebound-prone tech ETFs or single names above `watch_only` when volume is weak and MA60 remains unreclaimed.
- After repeated broad-index ETF bullish failures, require breadth, volume, and moving-average confirmation before another `buy_candidate` or `hold`.

## Updating This File
- Only add a rule after repeated evidence across multiple review windows.
- Keep rules short, testable, and operational.
