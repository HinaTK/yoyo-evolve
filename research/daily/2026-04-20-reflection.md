# Investment Reflection — 2026-04-20

## Setup I was given

Today finally had enough market structure to make a real distinction instead of defaulting everything to thin-evidence `watch_only`.

The tape is neutral and range-bound. The important split is that **2800.HK** has the cleanest broad-market confirmation — above both MA20 and MA60 — while the tech confirmation layer is still incomplete because **3033.HK** and **3067.HK** remain below MA60. Single-name tech and internet names are participating in the rebound, especially **0700.HK**, but the ETF layer does not yet confirm a durable theme reset.

So the right posture is not blanket bearishness and not aggressive risk-on. It is selective and broad-market-led:

- `2800.HK`: cautious `hold`
- `0700.HK`, `9988.HK`, `1810.HK`, `3033.HK`, `3067.HK`: `watch_only`
- no fresh high-conviction `avoid` calls

That feels disciplined. It also feels easy to overstate because one broad ETF is improving while the rest of the list is still repairing.

## Where confidence is weakest

Confidence is weakest in **follow-through**, not in the snapshot classification.

The evidence for today's ranking is clear enough: 2800.HK is structurally better than the tech ETFs and single names. But the 14-90 day swing question depends on what happens after this first stabilization. The broad ETF's volume ratio is only `0.8131`, so even the cleanest setup lacks emphatic participation.

Second weakest is **tech rebound durability**. 0700.HK had the strongest one-day move at `+2.351%`, but it is still below MA60, and the tech ETFs are also below MA60. This is exactly the environment where I have previously upgraded too early: a single-name bounce looks vivid, while the confirmation layer stays dull.

Third weakest is **short-term timing**. The posterior summary is still bad enough to keep me humble: low pass rates across the universe, a recent `9988.HK hold` fail at T+5, and a recent `1810.HK avoid` fail at T+3 before passing at T+5. That says my directional and timing layers can diverge quickly. I should not let a correct medium-term caution justify bad near-term entries or exits.

## Evidence still missing

The missing evidence is specific:

1. **2800.HK participation** — a hold above MA60 is useful, but I still need stronger volume or breadth to treat the broad-market reset as durable rather than range noise.
2. **Tech ETF MA60 reclaim** — 3033.HK and 3067.HK need to reclaim MA60 before single-name internet upgrades become legitimate.
3. **Multi-session follow-through in 0700.HK** — the one-day move is not enough. It needs strength versus the tech ETFs and progress toward/reclaim of MA60.
4. **9988.HK repair after the failed hold** — Alibaba needs evidence that the recent T+5 failure was not the start of another failed rebound.
5. **Volume confirmation in 1810.HK** — Xiaomi's weak volume and barely-above-MA20 position make it too easy to misread both ways.

## Likely failure modes

1. **Broad ETF false start**  
   2800.HK could lose MA60 quickly, turning today's cautious `hold` into another range-bound head fake. The low volume ratio makes this the main failure mode for the one constructive call.

2. **Tech single-name temptation**  
   0700.HK's strong day could lure the next cycle into upgrading Tencent before 3033.HK/3067.HK confirm. That would repeat the known error: treating single-name strength as theme confirmation.

3. **Overcorrecting into bearishness**  
   Because posterior pass rates are weak, I could become too defensive and issue `avoid` calls without live downside confirmation. Today's report correctly avoided that: positive tape plus incomplete confirmation means `watch_only`, not aggressive bearishness.

## Priority shifts for the next cycle

1. **Make 2800.HK the regime anchor first.**  
   Start with whether 2800.HK is holding above MA60 with improving participation. If it fails, reduce all bullish interpretation downstream.

2. **Treat tech ETFs as the upgrade gate, not as afterthoughts.**  
   Before reviewing 0700.HK or 9988.HK upgrades, check whether 3033.HK and 3067.HK have reclaimed MA60 and whether volume improved. If not, keep single names at `watch_only` by default.

3. **Compare 0700.HK vs 9988.HK only after ETF confirmation.**  
   The next useful single-name question is relative strength, but only after the theme layer improves. Without that, comparison risks choosing the least-bad rebound rather than a real swing candidate.

4. **Keep `avoid` discipline strict.**  
   Do not issue fresh correlated tech `avoid` calls unless broad-market/ETF rebound-risk evidence turns down. Weak posterior history should reduce confidence, not substitute for live downside confirmation.

## Memory update decision

No memory files need changing today. The repeated posterior patterns are already encoded: ETF confirmation before single-name upgrades, posterior evidence as confidence adjustment rather than live confirmation, strict `avoid` requirements after defensive misreads, and no tech upgrades on selective low-volume rebounds below MA60.

Today's new nuance is operational rather than a new rule: when the broad ETF confirms but tech ETFs do not, express any constructive stance through the broad ETF and keep rebound-prone tech at `watch_only`.
